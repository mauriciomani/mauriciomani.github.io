Acidity, tannins, sugar, alcohol and body... How to really understand what is going on?

I was in a free wine tasting the other day were we tried 3 Louis Latour wines, Ardeche chardonnay, Pouilly Fuisse, and Beaujolais-Villages. 
To be honest most of the folks we were there were completely out of the wine world and probably just there becuase of the free wine. 
However, some participants were trying to play the connoisseur, with words like fresh, vibrant, clean, easy-to-drink.
The cool thing was that we could compare Pouilly Fuisse and Ardeche, find the differences and the one we like the most.
Nothing to fancy but it made me doubt my senses, of course every one will have a different perceptions and appreciation and that is ok.
Nevertheless, I think you can like any wine you want, but you need to understand why you like the wine.
For example, I felt Ardeche alcoholic due to the lack of body, how dry it was and how low the acidity wine, 
but there were some folks describing it as fresh and vibrant. 
Then not understanding why Pouilly Fuisse was more fresh, with a full body, ending up in an elegant wine, 
making the alcohol very subtle compared to the Ardeche; basically I blame how dry Ardeche was. 
Even though is branded as a daily wine is not the type of wine that I would like to drink. 
So it made me thought about the importance of understanding wine components, so I setup an experiment.
